What is Meditation?

Meditation is a set of techniques that are intended to encourage a heightened state of awareness and focused attention. Meditation is also a consciousness technique that has been shown to have a wide number of benefits on psychological well-being.

The Basics of Meditation

  • Focuses the Mind: It is a common misconception that meditation causes the mind to become "quiet". In reality, your mind is always thinking. Although your thoughts might not be as rapid, it is perfectly nromal for your mind to be active when you are meditating. The key is to acknowledge this with compassion while bringing the focus back to the breath whenever you can. Meditation is like training a puppy to sit; the mind being the puppy, and the breath being the trainer.
  • Being in the Now: Rather than focusing on the past or the future, all meditative practices involve focusing on the present. Being in the now involves experiencing each moment, letting it go, and then experiencing the next. Focusing on the here and now takes practice, as many of us live most of our lives thinking towards the future of ruminating on the past.
  • Altered State of Consciousness: With time, maintaining a quiet mind and focusing on the present can lead to an altered level of consciousness that isn't asleeping state but isn't quite your average wakeful state either. Meditation increases brain acitivty in an area of the brain associated with happiness and positive thoughts and emotions, and some evidence shows that regular practice brings prolonged positive changes in these areas.

Meditation Techniques

This involves sitting in a comfortable position and using the breath as a point of focus. If you find yourself distracted by other thoughts of find your mind beginning to wander, gently redirect your focus back towards your breath.

#1 Focus Meditation




Focused meditation requires you to focus your attention without being distracted by your thoughts You can focus on something visual, like a statue. Some focus their attention on audiotory, like a recording of ocean waves; something constant like your breathing or your heart beating.

Many people find it easier to do this than focus on nothing, but the concept is the same - staying in the present moment, circumventing the constant stream of commentart from your conscious mind, and allowing yourself to slip into an altered state of consciousness.



#2 Activity Based Meditation




Activity based meditation combines meditation with activities that you new to you or activities that you enjoy. If you can teach yourself to focus on th epresent moment while partaking in these activities such as gardening, walking/hill walking, going for hike, or even yoga. This type of meditation can quiet the mind while you are "in the zone" experiencing the activity.

Activity based meditation technique is a great experience for beginners who want to try meditation with an activity they enjoy. These techniques are easier to calm th emind and focus on a particular activity. With regular practice, you should notice a difference in your physical and mental wellbeing



#3 Mindfulness Meditation




Mindfulnesss meditation similar to Activity Based Meditation, might not look like your idea of meditation. Mindfulness simply involves staying in the present rather than thinking about the future or the past. Again, this can be more difficult than it seems. Focusing on sensations you feel in your body is one way to stay "in the now". Focusing on emotions and where you feel them in your body - not examining why you feel them, but just experiencing them as sensations - is another



#4 Spiritual Meditation




Spiritual Meditation is not specific to any religion, it can be a spiritual practice. You can meditate on a singular question until an answer comes, or meditate to clear your mind and accept whatever comes that day. Many people also practice kundalini meditation for mind/body connection.

At it's centre, spiritual meditation is the practice of a mindful connection to something greater, than the individual. This may seem paradoxical but the path to that connection passes through honest self-reflection.



#5 Breathing Meditation




Breathing meditation is the most popular form of meditation because of its simplicity and convenience, as we are always breathing. The breath provides a natural focus that's unobstrusive, but always there. It also creates a natural rhythm in case you get lost.
